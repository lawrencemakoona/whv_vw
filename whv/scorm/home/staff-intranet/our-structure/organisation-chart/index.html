Organisation chart

  • 1: The chief executive officer (CEO) is the most senior member of an organisation. They are responsible for overseeing the organisation’s overall strategies, operations, and resources. Elected by the board of directors, the CEO is the main communication point between them and the rest of the organisation and public.
  • 2: The executive assistant provides administrative and internal and external communications support to the CEO.
  • 3: The business services manager supervises a team responsible for the business hub and recruitment services. They make sure that these services are properly equipped, are up-to-date, operate within budget, and meet community needs.
  • 4: The employment and training manager is the lead for a team dedicated to helping people develop the skills they need to be work-ready and employable.
  • 5: The community programs manager supervises a team that ensures that the GCBH programs are aligned to the needs of the community and values of the organisation and that they are running on time and on budget.
  • 6: The finance and administration manager heads a team that is responsible for GCBH’s business plan, budgeting for the organisation’s operations and programs and services, staff payroll, and filing taxes.
  • 7: The human resources manager leads a team responsible for all internal employee considerations and concerns. This includes managing and communicating staff benefits, training, compensation, and providing advice on any employee issues.
  • 8: The business liaison builds relationships with the community, working with local businesses to keep up-to-date on grants, sponsorships, and employment and training information and opportunities.
  • 9: The events coordinator is responsible for organising events for GCBH and for promoting the organisation. This includes planning, running, and evaluating workshops, seminars, and meetings.
  • 10: The receptionist manages communications and security with visitors. They answer and direct visitor inquiries to the appropriate parties and maintain a safe environment by following security procedures like logging and distributing visitor badges.
  • 11: The employment advisor works with people to help them identify and overcome barriers they may face when seeking and maintaining employment.
  • 12: The training coordinator consults with businesses to determine what skills are in demand. They help jobseekers map out skills development and training paths to achieve job and career goals.
  • 13: The community arts worker leads the arts education program at GCBH. They people excited and interested in community arts by learning different art techniques and styles and attending and sharing their work at local exhibits, festivals, and markets. They ensure the program reflects the diversity of the community and promotes positive mental health and living.
  • 14: The community sports worker oversees GCBH’s sports and recreation program. They promote and encourage people from the community to take part to develop skills and improve their health through physical activity and connecting with others.
  • 15: The accountant looks after GCBH’s financial records and operations. This includes writing the finance reports, filing tax returns, and managing accounts.
  • 16: The quality analyst is responsible for making sure GCBH’s programs and services meet community standards and the organisation’s visions and values.
  • 17: The administration support officer gives administrative and communications support to the finance and administration team.
  • 18: The HR support officer provides administrative and communications support to all employees for any questions, comments, or concerns they have regarding HR topics.
  • 19: The volunteer coordinator is responsible for recruiting, training, and managing volunteers.
  • 20: Volunteers are individuals who give their time and skills to the community arts and sports and recreation programs.
